India’s Push for ‘Made in India’ Tech – What It Means for Procurement Teams

For years, technology procurement in India often meant choosing between imported systems and the limited local options available.

In recent years however, the landscape has changed dramatically. The Government of India’s Make in India and Atmanirbhar Bharat initiatives are no longer just slogans, they’re shaping procurement policies, vendor preferences, and supply chains.

For procurement teams in corporates, government institutions, and large organisations, this shift raises important questions: What does “Made in India” actually mean? How does it impact procurement decisions? And what should you look out for when evaluating vendors?

Let’s break it down.

From Slogan to Directive

Government policies now actively prioritise locally manufactured products. This isn’t just symbolic:

  • Public Procurement (Preference to Make in India) Order gives preference to suppliers who meet minimum local content requirements.
  • Atmanirbhar Bharat campaign encourages reducing dependence on imports in key sectors.
  • In tenders, government departments often mandate or award additional points to vendors offering Made in India solutions.

This ripple effect extends into the private sector as well. Corporate procurement teams are finding that choosing Indian-made technology often aligns with client expectations, CSR priorities, and compliance considerations.

Why ‘Made in India’ Matters for Procurement

From a procurement manager’s perspective, the push toward indigenous solutions offers clear benefits:

1. Compliance and Eligibility

For government projects, compliance with “Make in India” requirements is non-negotiable. Even private sector tenders increasingly specify preference for local manufacturing, making it a baseline criterion.

2. Availability & Faster Service

Imported systems often face delays due to shipping, customs, or lack of local spares. Domestic manufacturers, by contrast, can offer:

  • Readily available spare parts
  • On-ground service teams
  • Quicker turnaround on repairs and replacements

This translates directly into lower downtime.

3. Cost Efficiency

Local sourcing reduces dependency on fluctuating forex rates and import duties. Over the lifecycle of a product, these savings can be significant, especially in large-scale deployments across multiple sites.

4. Trust & Accountability

With Indian vendors, audits, compliance checks, and communication are simpler. Decision-makers can more easily visit facilities, review processes, and hold vendors accountable, something that is much harder to do with overseas suppliers.

How Procurement Teams Can Evaluate Vendors

When shortlisting vendors in line with India’s push for domestic manufacturing, procurement teams should ask:

  • What percentage of the product is actually manufactured in India?
    Look for transparency in the supply chain.
  • Are the products certified to meet both Indian and international standards?
    Certifications like ISO, BIS, and STQC add credibility.
  • What’s the after-sales support infrastructure?
    Local presence and quick response matter as much as the product itself.
  • Does the vendor have proven experience with scale?
    For projects involving thousands of users or multiple locations, scalability is non-negotiable.
  • What’s the Total Cost of Ownership (TCO)?
    Evaluate not just purchase cost, but maintenance, downtime, and upgrades.
Building Long-Term Resilience

The bigger picture is this: by choosing credible Made in India vendors, procurement teams aren’t just ticking a policy box, they’re contributing to a more resilient ecosystem.

  • For government institutions, this means smoother rollouts and better alignment with national priorities.
  • For corporates, it translates into reduced risk, improved support, and stronger alignment with ESG and CSR goals.

In sectors like biometric attendance, access control, and POS systems, where reliability, service, and scale matter, local expertise built around Indian conditions often outperforms imported one-size-fits-all solutions.

Wrapping Up

India’s push for “Made in India” technology is more than a patriotic initiative. It’s changing the way procurement teams evaluate vendors, balance costs, and ensure compliance.

For decision-makers, the key is to go beyond labels and evaluate vendors on transparency, reliability, and long-term value. Done right, sourcing locally isn’t just about compliance, it’s about building partnerships that support growth and resilience.
