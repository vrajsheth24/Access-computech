Big retail tech made accessible for local kirana stores

Kirana stores are still going strong, even with big retail and then quick commerce trying to disrupt the sector. But how long can they continue with the growing difference in technology used by these chains?

The juggernaut of supermarkets and e-commerce is yet to disrupt the network of millions of humble Kirana stores spread across India. These stores, mostly family-run or individually owned, operate within residential areas and often offer credit to regular customers. They are usually small in size and offer a wide array of products like groceries, snacks, toiletries, and other household items. Marked by personalised service and local trust, a staggering 86% of Indian households still prefer to buy their daily groceries from local Kirana stores, says a 2022 Axis my India survey. And why wouldn’t they? Kirana stores feature in most of our childhood memories and still serve as centers where people of the neighborhood buy their daily supplies and fondly meet each other.

A silent struggle

Though competition with modern forms of business is yet to significantly affect Kirana stores their major challenge comes in the form of late adoption of basic technologies like Point Of Sale systems that fuels the silent struggles of many kirana store owners. The lives of many such store-owners are marked by:

  • Long hours filled with manual work and calculation errors.
  • Frustration from stockouts or unsold goods.
  • Missed opportunities to earn more or grow.
  • Fear of theft or revenue leakage.

Shouldn’t these small businesses, that have served as the backbone of the Indian economy for more than 75 years, be given the chance to exist in the face of big corporations? Can’t they still be a useful part of our neighborhoods with a few minor changes?
