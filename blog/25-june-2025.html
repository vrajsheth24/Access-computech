Secure Your Premises With Multispectral Biometrics

Multi-Spectral biometric technology isn't just the future, it's the present and Access Computech is ready to help you implement it.

It's a normal Monday morning and you're running a high-security facility with a traditional access control system which just failed to recognise your security head because he cut his finger or maybe an employee can't clock in because his hands are wet from rain. Well that's definitely a bad way to start the week.

This is where multi-spectral biometric technology comes in and today we're diving deep into how Access Computech is revolutionising security with technology that literally reads beneath the surface.

What is Multi-Spectral Biometric technology?

Let's start with the basics. Have you ever noticed how your smartphone’s fingerprint scanner sometimes fails when your finger is slightly wet or dirty? That is because it only reads the surface of your skin.

Multi-Spectral biometric technology is completely different. Instead of looking just at your fingertip’s surface, it uses multiple wavelengths of light to penetrate up to 4000 micrometers deep into your skin. That's about four times deeper than traditional optical scanners.

Another simple analogy will be this. Think of a traditional fingerprint scanner as taking a photo of a painting, multi-spectral scanning is like having an x-ray vision that sees through the paint to the canvas underneath.

This technology captures what we call subsurface fingerprint data, which means it can read your unique biometric signature even when your surface skin is compromised. Cuts, scars, dirt, moisture or even gloves don't matter anymore.

Why is Multi-Spectral technology suited the best for High-security environments?

Traditional biometric systems fail in real-world conditions about 15-20% of the time. This might not sound like much, but in a facility with 1000 employees, that's 150-200 of your employees who can't get access to their workplace on any given day.

This problem gets worse in industrial environments. Workers with callused hands, employees who work with chemicals that can damage skin, or anyone in a wet environment, they all struggle with traditional scanners.

But, here's the kicker. The global biometric system market is projected to reach $84.5 billion by 2029, growing at a CAGR of 12.3%. Companies are clearly investing heavily in biometric security, but they require technology that actually works efficiently in the real world.

This is exactly the problem that we at Access Computech have been solving for over 25+ years. We've seen every challenge you can imagine and our Multi-Spectral solutions address them all.

Game changing benefits

Let's talk about the benefits that this technology actually delivers.

  • Accuracy under all conditions: While traditional scanners might fail 15-20% of the time in challenging conditions, multi-spectral scanners maintain over 99% accuracy even with damaged or compromised fingerprints.
  • Spoof and fake-finger detection: The Lumidigm sensors don't just read fingerprints, they actively verify that they're scanning living tissue. This means silicone molds, latex gloves or other spoofing attempts simply won't work.
  • These devices are built for harsh environments. With IP55 ratings and battery backups, they keep working when other systems fail. We're talking technology that can handle industrial sites, outdoor installations and extreme weather conditions.
  • High throughput processing: These systems can match templates against databases of up to 100,000 fingerprints per second. That's industry grade high-level performance.
  • Compliance and Certifications: Access Computech holds ISO 9001, 14000 and 27001 certifications, we're CMM Level 3 and we have BIS Certification. Our devices are Genetac compatible, which means seamless integration with existing security infrastructure.

Frequently Asked Questions
Will multi-spectral technology still work if a fingerprint is worn, cut or dirty?

Yes. These scanners capture subsurface details up to 4000 micrometers deep, making them highly effective and accurate even with damaged surface prints.

How does spoof detection feature actually prevent fake fingerprints?

Access Computech devices use Lumidigm MSI sensors that actively distinguish live skin from artificial materials by analysing multiple light spectra. It's not just reading the pattern, it's verifying that it's attached to a person.

Can these readers be used offline or in remote sites?

Absolutely. The Sentinel FP, Sentinel PROX MF Dual and AST 300 all offer offline mode with internal storage and battery backup. They can operate independently and sync data later when connectivity is restored.

Will the system integrate with existing security infrastructure?

Yes. We provide Genetac-certified integration, enabling seamless connection with your existing surveillance infrastructure and more.
