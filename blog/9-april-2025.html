What Restaurants Want from POS Systems in 2025

In 2025, running a successful restaurant isn’t limited to what’s on the plate, it’s about how quickly and efficiently everything runs behind the scenes.

(Data Sources: https://www.hospitalitynet.org/opinion/4124700.html)

A great meal can bring people in, but a smooth experience (including the checkout) keeps them coming back. People will tend to not return even if they had the best burger in town but had to wait 20 minutes just to pay for it.

People like speed with flexibility

Nobody likes waiting, whether it’s for food, the bill, or a server to take their order. Especially waiting for the bill when they’re full and itching to continue with their day’s plans. Diners today expect speed and convenience, and the numbers prove it. According to the 2024 Customer Engagement Technology Study, 66% of diners prefer ordering from their phones, 58% want to pay the same way, and 32% of full-service restaurant customers would rather settle their bill with a table-side device than flag someone down.

If your POS system can’t handle these modern expectations, you’re already falling behind. Customers want options in everything, whether it’s ordering through an app, scanning a QR code, or using a handheld device at the table. And the faster they can order and pay, the quicker your table turnover, which means more business.

A POS is for more than payments nowadays

A restaurant’s POS system isn’t just a fancy cash register, it should be doing some heavy lifting behind the scenes. According to the 2024 POS Software Trends Study, 84% of restaurant owners plan to add new features to their POS systems to improve service and efficiency. And it’s not hard to see why.

Think about inventory. No one wants to hear, "Sorry, we're out of that," after they’ve already set their heart on a dish. A good POS system keeps track of stock in real-time, so menus update automatically when something runs out. That way, customers aren’t ordering something you can’t serve, and your staff isn’t scrambling to explain why.

Then there’s loyalty programs. People love discounts, and 82% of diners say they’d be more likely to return if they earned rewards. A smart POS should integrate loyalty programs directly with no extra apps and no complicated steps. Just simple, automatic points and discounts that keep customers coming back.

And let’s not forget integration. Running a restaurant means juggling multiple systems—reservations, payroll, CRM, online ordering. The last thing you need is a POS that refuses to play nice with everything else. But here’s the problem: 44% of restaurant owners say their current systems struggle to connect with new software. That’s a big deal because a disconnected system means more manual work, more errors, and more time wasted fixing things that shouldn’t be broken in the first place.

Data will actually help you make better decisions

A smart POS doesn’t just process transactions, it tells you what’s working and what’s not. It tracks sales trends, so you know which dishes are a hit and which ones should probably retire. It helps with staffing decisions by showing peak hours and slow periods, so you’re not over or understaffed. And when you have real-time reports at your fingertips, making better business decisions becomes a whole lot easier.

Upgrading existing systems should be easier

Even with all these benefits, plenty of restaurant owners hesitate to upgrade their systems. And fair enough, switching to a new POS sounds like a hassle. No one wants downtime, lost data, or a confused staff trying to figure out new software while customers are waiting.

That’s why choosing the right POS provider matters. A well-designed system should be easy to implement without disrupting operations. It should reduce labor costs by automating routine tasks, speed up ordering, and eliminate unnecessary steps in the payment process. And most importantly, it should come with support that actually helps, not just a chatbot that sends you to the FAQ page.
